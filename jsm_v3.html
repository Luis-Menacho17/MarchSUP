SPMT & SSST — whole-school support frameworks
Two coordinated structures that ensure no student or staff member falls through the cracks
👥 SPMT — School Planning & Management Team
The SPMT serves as our school’s strategic planning body — bringing together school leadership, staff, families, and community partners to set direction, review data, and ensure our plans are aligned to student and school needs. It is the engine of our continuous improvement cycle.
🩹 SSST — School Supportive Services Team
The SSST coordinates wraparound support for students with academic, behavioral, or social-emotional needs. The team — including the counselor, social worker, and administrators — meets regularly to review referrals, develop intervention plans, and ensure every student has access to the support they need to be present and ready to learn.

⚠ Impeding factors
What is getting in the way of full implementation and progress
Class size above district average
JSM class sizes are above the district average, which creates challenges for differentiated instruction — particularly given that 51% of students are multilingual learners and 14.4% have IEPs. Larger classes make it harder for teachers to provide the individualized attention our students need.
Capacity
Title I tutoring budget limits our ability to act on targeting data — we know which students need it, but don't have the staffing to deliver it at scale.
Staffing
FMLA absences and inconsistent substitute coverage disrupt instructional continuity — particularly in grades with high ML or IEP concentrations.
ML Equity
JSM has added an additional TESOL teacher and paraeducator to better support our multilingual learners — a meaningful investment. However, ML literacy support structures still fade after Grade 3, where 41–69% of ML students in Grades 4–6 remain in the lowest NWEA reading band. A district-funded bridge-to-English model for upper grades is still needed.
IEP Load & Enrollment
72 students with IEPs (14.4%) — and growing. Students are being enrolled at JSM without advance identification of support needs; many arrive mid-year with existing IEPs, expanding caseloads with no corresponding staffing increase. Grades 3–5 IEP students: 70–100% in the lowest NWEA reading band.
Budget
Proposed FY 26–27 budget of $4,295,123 is $174,821 above 25–26 actuals ($4,120,302), driven by personnel costs. Non-personnel — which covers intervention materials and tutoring — is only $181,985 of the total. Any reduction or flat allocation will directly cut intervention capacity.
Families
Immigration-related fears are keeping families away from evening and afterschool events. Family climate survey response was 9% — below any meaningful threshold for data-informed decisions.
Technology
Apptegy onboarding is still incomplete. Many families have not fully transitioned from Class Dojo — communication gaps are a direct contributor to low family engagement metrics.
